Five years ago, when I was going into college, I bought a Kuhl Burr jacket in gunmetal. 
This thing is my favorite jacket. It's a distinctive part of my silhouette. I also want
to replace it. I'm gonna spend a lot of time talking about it. I'm not
being sponsored, I just really like this thing and also wish it was better made. 
 

## I like the style
The things I love about this jacket: the color, the durable metal zippers, the style in
general. The sleeves are constructed out of way more panels than you really need, but
each flat-sewn French seam is flanked with lighter thread that accentuates the panels. 
It looks great. The front left zipper pocket is where I put either my sunglasses or my EDC
flashlight, gloves in the side pockets, and usually a mask in the inside pocket. I *love* the
fact that every pocket on this jacket is zippered. Lots of jackets have outer pockets with 
buttoned flaps, or even no flaps
at all. Pockets with two buttons, you're on thin ice. All of these pockets feel useless to 
me because, I don't trust things to stay in them. Zippered pockets are something this
jacket does better than a lot of more expensive jackets. f

The jacket is lined, with smoother fabric in the sleeves. It's polyester, which is to be
expected at this price point. It's not like you'd be getting silk in a $150 jacket. 

## I don't like the construction
- "Vintage patina dye" is not friction fast when wet
- Seems like it's trying to emulate waxed canvas jackets without actually needing to be as sturdy as one
- Weft is disintegrating after only 5 years
- Cuffs assembled with fusible interfacing :(

## Warranty Process
Kuhl wouldn't warranty the jacket for the aforementioned issues. To be fair, it *was*
outside their warranty period, but their warranty is only 18 months. For a jacket that
costs $150, I'd hope for a little more.
